TOPIOS: Tracking of Plastic In Our Seas

TOPIOS is a 5-year project, funded through an European Research Council Startig Grant project to Erik van Sebille, to vastly improve our understanding of the way plastic litter moves through our ocean.

The TOPIOS Team

Erik van Sebille

Lead and coordinator

Erik is Associate Professor in Oceanography and Climate Change at IMAU, Utrecht University, Netherlands, and is an expert in Lagrangian oceanography. He leads and coordinates the TOPIOS project.

Philippe Delandmeter

Ocean model developer

Philippe is a postdoc at IMAU, Utrecht University, Netherlands. His research focusses on improving an optimising the Parcels code used in TOPIOS to simulate plastic transport in the ocean.

David Wichmann

Physical oceanographer

David is a PhD student at IMAU, Utrecht University, Netherlands. He investigates the physics of how plastic litter is transported by currents and waves.

Victor Onink

Physical oceanographer

Victor is an MSc student at IMAU, Utrecht University, Netherlands. He investigates why floating plastic litter ends up in the infamous garbage patches.

Jose Alsina

Collaborator on wave flume experiments

Jose is a Lecturer in Fluid Mechanics at Imperial College London in the UK. He is a collaborator on the wave flume experiments.
